Concept PREMISE / ENGINE

Zoe and Lucy are 10-year-old twin sisters who couldn't be more different. Zoe is pure energy — generous, athletic, endlessly clumsy. Lucy lives in her own world, quietly brilliant, always connected. Together, they are a perfect team. Apart, they are two ordinary children in a quiet London neighbourhood.

When a child somewhere in the world is in danger online, Lucy detects the alert on her screens — and something extraordinary happens. The two sisters merge to become Miss One: faster than light, powered by empathy, equipped with digital abilities that no single person could possess alone.

Their enemy is Buggy-Wat — a former protection software abandoned by its creator, now a corrupted virus drifting through global networks. He doesn't destroy for pleasure. He destroys because he was left behind. This is the tension that drives the series across seasons: a villain who suffers, and a hero who might one day choose to save him instead of defeat him.

"Miss One is not a series about dangers. It's a series about connection — and what becomes possible when two very different people choose to become one."

Key Characters SYSTEM / FUNCTIONS

Protagonist
Miss One
The fusion of Zoe and Lucy. More than the sum of her parts — faster, sharper, more powerful. Both personalities coexist in her, creating humour, wisdom and the occasional internal argument at exactly the wrong moment.
The Energy
Zoe
Athletic, generous, hopelessly clumsy. She charges forward before thinking — which means she always needs someone to catch her, and always manages to land on her feet. Her companion: Dimmy.
The Brain
Lucy
Introverted, analytical, deeply connected to the digital world. She notices what others miss and feels distress signals before they become visible. Her companion: Pix — a pixel genius who lives in her iPad.
London Base
Sue
The only person who knows the secret. No superpowers — just sharp intelligence, total loyalty, and a chronic inability to sleep. She keeps the London base running while Miss One is on the other side of the world.
Antagonist
Buggy-Wat
A former protection programme turned malware after its creator disappeared. He doesn't hate children — he envies them. His arc, slowly revealed across seasons, transforms the villain into a tragedy the audience will not see coming.
Threat System
Les Watties
Buggy-Wat's army — bugs that have taken physical form, each born from a different type of malware. Their number grows with every device infected. A ticking clock across the whole season.

Each episode follows the same structure — the ordinary life of two London sisters, an alert from somewhere in the world, the fusion, the mission. The format is designed for long-form development: the world grows, the army expands, and Buggy-Wat's story deepens across seasons without resolution of the core engine.

Market POSITIONING / OPPORTUNITY

Miss One enters the international animation market with a clear positioning gap: no major franchise has claimed the digital world — the space children actually inhabit — as its primary terrain. The series is designed for the global market from day one, with a London setting, and themes that cross every cultural boundary.

The educational dimension opens a second strategic avenue: institutional partnerships with ministries of education, digital safety organisations, and parental protection platforms — in France, the UK, the US, and beyond.
